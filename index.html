Our Mission

Protecting Earth's Natural Heritage

National Parks are the jewels of our planet — vast wilderness areas set aside for the protection of natural ecosystems, wildlife habitats, and cultural landscapes. This portal brings you closer to these magnificent spaces.

From dense rainforests to sweeping savannas, from towering mountain ranges to pristine coral reefs, national parks preserve the world's most extraordinary environments for generations to come.

Yellowstone National Park

Wyoming, USA

The world's first national park, famous for geysers, hot springs, and abundant wildlife including bison and grizzly bears.

🐻 Bears 🦬 Bison 🌋 Geysers

Serengeti National Park

Tanzania, Africa

Witness the greatest wildlife spectacle on Earth — the annual Great Migration of over 1.5 million wildebeest across the plains.

🦁 Lions 🐘 Elephants 🦒 Giraffes
Entry: $70/day

Brazil ⭐ 4.8

Amazon National Park

Amazonas, Brazil

The lungs of the Earth — a vast tropical rainforest harboring millions of species of insects, plants, birds, and other forms of life.

🐆 Jaguars 🦜 Macaws 🌴 Rainforest

India ⭐ 4.8

Jim Corbett National Park

Uttarakhand, India

India's oldest national park, renowned for Bengal tigers, Asian elephants, and over 600 species of birds in the Himalayan foothills.

Wildlife

Amazing Wildlife

Meet the extraordinary creatures that call these parks home.

Mammal
African Lion
The king of the African savanna, found in Serengeti and Kruger National Parks. Lions live in prides and are apex predators.
Mammal
Bengal Tiger
The world's largest wild cat, endangered and protected in India's Jim Corbett and Ranthambore National Parks.
Mammal
African Elephant
Earth's largest land animal, a keystone species in African ecosystems. Found in Serengeti, Amboseli, and Kruger Parks.
Bird
Bald Eagle
The symbol of America, soaring above Yellowstone and the Pacific Northwest, with a wingspan up to 8 feet wide.
Mammal
White-Tailed Deer
One of the most graceful park residents, found throughout North American national parks including Yellowstone and Smoky Mountains.
Bird
Flamingo
These stunning pink birds are found in salty and alkaline lakes. Famous flocks gather at Lake Nakuru, Kenya.
Mammal
Red Fox
Highly adaptable and cunning, red foxes are found across multiple continents in parks ranging from temperate to arctic.
Primate
Mountain Gorilla
Critically endangered, mountain gorillas are protected in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest in Uganda and Virunga National Park.

Who We Are

Guardians of Nature's Legacy

The National Parks Portal was created to bridge the gap between modern technology and the ancient wonders of our natural world. We believe that digital access to park information inspires real-world conservation.

Our platform replaced outdated manual record systems — paper files that were vulnerable to damage, loss, and inefficiency. Today, all park data is stored securely, searchable instantly, and accessible from anywhere on the globe.

From the geysers of Yellowstone to the jungles of the Amazon, we bring every corner of the wild world to your fingertips — so you can plan your visit, learn about wildlife, and join the conservation movement.

History

Timeline of Conservation

Key milestones in the global national parks movement.

1872
Yellowstone — World's First
The United States established Yellowstone as the first national park in the world, setting a global precedent for conservation.
1916
US National Park Service Founded
The NPS was established to manage and protect a growing system of parks, monuments, and other natural areas across America.
1948
Serengeti Protected
Tanzania's Serengeti became a protected area, safeguarding the world's greatest wildlife migration for future generations.
1972
World Heritage Convention
UNESCO's World Heritage Convention created a framework to identify and protect natural and cultural sites of outstanding universal value.
2000
Digital Era Begins
National park systems worldwide began digitizing records, enabling online access to park information for the first time.
2024
National Parks Portal Launched
This portal was created to provide comprehensive, searchable, and map-enabled digital access to world national park information.
